# Finding Rad Idea Bank

A working bank of episode ideas for the channel.

Principle: save strong concepts while they're hot, then rank them by hook, buildability, and how well they fit the Finding Rad thesis.

## Current Priority Stack

### 1. Builder Episode 1 — This, That, or Do It!
**Hook:** I built a kids learning app that forces my kids to move instead of just stare at a screen.

**Why it works:**
- Real problem, real use case, real kids
- Strong emotional hook as a dad building for his own boys
- Visual and physical, which makes it instantly more watchable
- Great "build in public" energy

**Episode structure:**
1. Cold open with the kids using it
2. Problem: kids apps are usually just more screen time
3. Excalidraw core loop whiteboard
4. Show the repo and current state
5. Build one new feature live
6. End with the boys testing it

**What makes people stay:**
- The reaction shot from the kids
- Watching the app go from concept to working product
- The mix of parenting, design, and AI building

**Status:** Active / best candidate for Builder Episode 1

---

### 2. Builder Episode 2 — DoubleDog
**Hook:** We built a dare app for adults. Here's how the whole thing works.

**Why it works:**
- Same builder format, different audience
- Competitive/social mechanic is easy to explain
- Good follow-up after the kids app because it shows range

**Episode structure:**
1. Explain the core dare/stakes loop
2. Whiteboard the app logic
3. Show the current product and repo
4. Build or refine a live feature on camera
5. End with a real test flow

**Risk:**
- Needs the product to feel tight enough on camera
- Less emotionally universal than the kids app

**Status:** Strong Builder Episode 2 option

### 3. How AI Runs a Physical Product Company
**Hook:** I don't have a big team. I have AI.

**Why it works:**
- Broader than a coding audience
- Useful to founders, operators, and small business owners
- Hibear gives instant credibility because it's a real business

**Episode structure:**
1. Explain the problem of running too many functions with too little time
2. Show the stack: ads, email, CS, content, Amazon, ops
3. Walk through real workflows and automations
4. Show where AI genuinely helps and where it still sucks
5. End with the honest scorecard

**Why this could travel:**
- It solves a real founder problem
- Easy to share: "watch how he runs Hibear with AI"

**Status:** Best non-builder episode candidate

---

### 4. I Built My YouTube Channel's Website With AI
**Hook:** I built findingrad.com with AI, and it turns my videos into real editorial blog posts.

**Why it works:**
- Meta in a good way
- Directly relevant to the Finding Rad audience
- Lets Mark show taste, systems thinking, and shipping speed

**Episode structure:**
1. Start with why the channel needs a home outside YouTube
2. Show the architecture: transcript → Claude → blog post → site
3. Walk through the repo, worker, and publishing flow
4. Build one live improvement on camera
5. End by showing the live site and latest post

**Risk:**
- Needs strong storytelling so it does not become a dry dev walkthrough

**Status:** Strong tutorial / builder crossover episode

---

### 5. Build Something Brand New Live
**Hook:** Let's go from app idea to working prototype in one episode.

**Why it works:**
- Raw and high-energy
- Strong tension because there is no safety net
- Fits the Finding Rad thesis perfectly

**Episode structure:**
1. Pick an idea on camera
2. Whiteboard it quickly
3. Define the visual direction
4. Build it live with AI tools
5. Demo what works at the end

**Risk:**
- Highest variance
- Can feel messy if the idea is weak or the build stalls

**Status:** High-upside format, better once audience trusts the process

## Workflow Principle
Do not copy someone else's exact workflow.
Steal the principle, not the recipe.

**Finding Rad workflow:**
1. Start with the story/problem
2. Pull visual references
3. Sketch the loop
4. Define the vibe and visual system
5. Build fast with AI tools

This keeps the process honest, creative, and native to the channel.

## Ranking Lens
When choosing the next episode, score each idea on:
- **Hook strength** — would someone click even if they do not know Mark?
- **Emotional stake** — is there a real reason to care?
- **Visual payoff** — can people see progress on screen?
- **Teachability** — does the viewer leave with something useful?
- **Series value** — does it make them want Episode 3?
